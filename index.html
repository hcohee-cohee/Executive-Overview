Consistent Strength
Culture grounding landed. Every time.
Informed Captaincy, Big Bets, Decision Making@ — attendees left knowing what Netflix values and how it operates. Both days delivered on cultural orientation.
⟳
Repeat Request
More interaction. Less lecture.
Both days received consistent requests for more table exercises, role-plays, and peer dialogue. Passive listening doesn't activate behavior — and attendees know it.
⏱
Timing Gap
Too late in tenure. Too long a day.
Multiple respondents on both days noted arriving 3–5 months in. Content hits hardest in month 1–2. Day 1 length was also frequently cited as exhausting.
→
Clear Ask
Give us something to take home.
Resource summaries, Slack cohorts, and follow-up nudges were requested unprompted by both Day 1 and Day 2 attendees. The appetite for continuity is real and unmet.

What We Did Not Yet Deliver

5 Gaps That Carry Into Q2

01
Repeat Gap
No failure stories. Either day.
Every speaker delivered a highlight reel. Attendees asked — for the second quarter in a row — for real missteps and uncomfortable truths. We're inspiring. We're not yet honest.
02
Repeat Gap
Zero post-event continuity shipped.
No resource packet. No Slack cohort. No 30/60/90 nudges. This was flagged in Q4. It remains unresolved. NEC ends when the doors close — and that's the problem.
03
Content Gap
Non-P&T functions feel peripheral.
Legal (10), Finance (44), Global Comms (3) attended — but Big Bets framing skews tech-heavy. No bridge connects strategy to their day-to-day reality.
04
Leadership Gap
VP+ attendance: 0 of 5 invited.
All 4 Vice Presidents and 1 Officer invited to the Q1 NEC cycle did not attend — either day. New hires and new managers notice when senior leadership doesn't model what they expect of others.
05
Structure Gap
One coordinator. 540+ attendees. No backup.
NEC operates as a single-point-of-failure. One person holds cross-functional coordination, execution, and institutional knowledge. This is now a program risk, not a staffing preference.
→
All 5 Have Owners
These are not observations. They are commitments.
Each gap has a named owner, a due date, and an action in the Q2 tracker. See the Top 5 Priorities below. No vague owners. No "we'll explore."
