Q2 Action

Top 5 Priorities for Q2

01
Run two cohorts of Day 1 in Q2 so no eligible employee is waitlisted or turned away.
Q2 demand is projected to exceed single-day Palladium capacity. Running the same Day 1 program across two separate dates — same content, same experience — ensures every new hire gets their NEC moment without compromising quality or access.
Target: Q2 Planning Complete by March 2026
02
Assign a dedicated owner to each day so execution accountability is never shared and never unclear.
Day 1 (All-Employee) and Day 2 (Leaders) are distinct programs with different audiences, content, and logistics. Each should have one primary owner who sees it from invite to debrief — reducing handoff risk and building deeper day-specific expertise.
Target: Owners confirmed by March 2026
03
Redesign the post-event email to drive survey completion — shorter survey, fewer links, QR at closing.
Day 1 saw a 30% survey abandonment rate among people who clicked the link. Day 2's 40% completion rate was driven by in-room distribution, not email. For Q2: 5-question max survey, one featured CTA link, and a QR code on the final slide with 3 minutes of quiet fill-in time before dismissal.
Target: New survey template ready before Q2 event
04
Add a "Pivots & Progress" segment to both days featuring a real challenge Netflix navigated.
Attendees consistently asked for honest stories — not just wins. A short, structured segment where a speaker shares what changed, why, and what was learned gives new employees the cultural permission to fail forward. This is the single highest-impact content change for Q2.
Target: Segment brief drafted 6 weeks before Q2
05
Build a cross-functional content bridge so Legal, Finance, and non-P&T functions see themselves in the program.
82% of Day 1 attendees are ICs, and 50% are from P&T — but Legal, Finance, and Global Comms make up a meaningful share of the room. Current Big Bets framing skews tech-heavy. One additional slide or facilitated moment that connects strategy to non-P&T functions would significantly increase relevance for a large portion of attendees.
Target: Content review complete 4 weeks before Q2
The Bottom Line
More Candor.
More Learning.
More Impact.
Q1 Result 465 + 75 attended. 98.1% and 92.6% show-up. 4.31 and 4.49 satisfaction. Strong execution. Strong production. Real culture delivered at scale.
The Gap Limited visibility into real pivots. Limited continuity after the room. Limited senior leadership presence. Attendees leave inspired — but not fully activated. Q2 is designed to close that gap.
Q2 Mandate Two cohorts run in parallel so no eligible employee is turned away. Each day has a single dedicated program owner — clear accountability, no single point of failure. Post-event touchpoints extend the experience beyond the room, so NEC becomes the beginning of the cultural journey, not the end of it.

What to change for Q2
01 · Survey
Shorten to 5 questions — 3 minutes max
Keep the 5 Likert ratings (they're fast and give you trend data quarter over quarter). Cut open-text from 3 to 1: "What's one thing you'd change?" That alone eliminates the 30% abandonment. Target: 97 completions for D1 instead of 68 — a 43% improvement with zero additional outreach.
02 · Distribution
Put a QR code on the closing slide for Day 1
Day 2's 40% response rate was driven by people filling it out in or immediately after the room — not by email. The highest motivation moment is right as the event ends. A QR code on the final slide + 3 minutes of quiet fill-in time before dismissal could add 60–80 responses on the spot for Day 1.
03 · Email
Cut Day 2 email to 3 links max
Day 2's email had 17 links. 13 got zero clicks. That's not a resource library — it's noise that buries your most important CTA (the survey). For Q2: survey link, one curated Learn resource, and PlusPlus. Everything else belongs in a separate "resource digest" email sent a week later to anyone who clicks "want more."
